About me

I was born and raised in Warendorf, a cute little town somewhere in Germany. It's a great place to live if you're into horseback riding, but not so much if you're interested in physics. So I moved to Munich at the age of 18, and did some cool physics there. I started out in astrophysics, and did two incredibly fun research internships: One at the Max Planck Institute for Astrophysics and one at the Nicolaus Copernicus Astronomical Center. After my four years of undergrad, it was time for me to explore new places, and so I moved to Cambridge (MA) to do my PhD.

In my free time, I like to try to play the piano and hang out with friends. I also like hiking, snowboarding, squirrels, blueberries, other berries, the number tau, and food. I don't like pi.

Pokédex

I finally managed to get my hands on a game boy color after many years of being deprived of one of my favorite Pokémon games. Considering that the innovative feature of the game boy color is (surprise, surprise) that it can display colors, the game feels incredinly black-and-white (which is awesome). Also, I love the fact that this game boy - unlike its immediate predecessor - does not have backlight, so there is no hope of seeing anything on this little screen unless one sits at a suitable angle with respect to a lamp.

Now I am on a quest to complete my Pokédex in the yellow version of Pokémon. I'll let you know how it's going (currently: 117 seen, 70 caught (out of 150)). Let me know if you are in Cambridge and interested in trading Pokémon!
