Hi, I'm Verena!

I am a graduate student in physics at MIT's Center for Theoretical Physics - a Leinweber Institute, working with Prof. David Kaiser.

My research interests lie at the intersection of theoretical cosmology and high-energy physics, with a focus on primordial black holes. You can find some information about my past projects on the Research page of this website.

Before MIT, I obtained my bachelor's degree in physics from the Technical University of Munich, with a thesis on lattice QCD and machine learning. I also did undergraduate research in astrophysics.
